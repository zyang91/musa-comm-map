Map 3: Redesign a Past Map
Go back to a map you made in a previous course (MUSA 5080 or another class). Redesign it using everything you have learned this semester. This is your before-and-after moment.

What to Do
Include your original map exactly as you submitted it. Do not modify it. This is the “before.”
Create a redesigned version of the same map. Use the same underlying data (or as close as possible), but apply the design principles from this course. At minimum, address: projection, classification method, color palette, legend design, title, clutter, and whether the map needs additional context layers or annotations.
Write a reflection (5–8 sentences) that answers all three of these questions:
What specific design problems does the original have?
What changes did you make and why? Be specific about each decision.
Who is the audience for this map, and what is the one thing you want them to take away? Write this as a Big Idea statement.
 

A Score of 3 Looks Like
The before-and-after difference is dramatic and clearly attributable to specific, named design principles. The reflection shows genuine understanding of why the original was weak and how each change serves the audience. The Big Idea statement is specific, actionable, and audience-aware.
